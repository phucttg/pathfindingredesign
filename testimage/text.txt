Overall, the project was partially successful as both a software artefact and a scoped educational design study. At the artefact level, the project successfully delivered an interactive pathfinding visualiser, with integrated support for explanation and pacing control. At the technical level, the artefact was supported with limitations, since core behaviours were tested but full browser-level assurance was not established. In this case, the project met the first objective and partially met the second and third objectives. However, the educational evidence remained more limited. Across the survey findings, positive patterns were observed in the pre-post responses, learning method preference items, and post-use questions. However, the inferential evidence remained weak, and the confidence intervals crossed zero. In addition, there was no control group or objective mastery testing. For this reason, the fourth objective can only be judged as partially met. In this sense, the evidence supports a scoped claim of educational promise. It does not support a strong confirmatory claim of broad learning gain. More specifically, the research questions were answered within the stated scope of the study rather than in a strongly confirmatory way. RQ1 showed a supportive but non-confirmatory pattern in perceived understanding, confidence, and interpretability. RQ2 suggested that the Insight Panel and learner-controlled playback and replay features were useful for inspection and reflection, although their individual effects were not isolated. RQ3 highlighted that the tool appeared stronger at supporting inspectability, confidence, and explanation accessibility as scaffolded interpretive support for novice learners than at supporting deeper rule-based understanding. Taken together, this tool appears educationally promising within the scope of this study. However, the evidence is not strong enough to demonstrate that the visualiser is an educationally effective approach or that it surpasses other methods.
